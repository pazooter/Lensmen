# Lensmen
Lensmen is an investigative analysis tool. You submit a claim, question, or piece of evidence, and Lensmen applies 16 structured analytical lenses to find nuttinesses — things in the record that don't add up, contradict each other, or sit oddly against what should be true. Every finding points somewhere specific. Nothing is left as a dead end.
The Five Steps
Step 1 — Submit Your Claim
Type or paste what you want investigated into the claim field.
NOTE: Lensmen works best on subjects that have a public or documentable record — events, institutions, public figures, published statements, media reports, historical episodes, organizational behaviour. For questions where the only available data is what you personally hold — a private dispute, a missing object, an undocumented incident — supply that data directly in the claim field and note that no external record exists. Hit Submit ↗.
Step 2 — Confirm the Ideal Scene
Lensmen derives an Ideal Scene — a single sentence stating the best positive, achievable condition implied by your claim. This is the baseline everything will be measured against.
Read it. If the inference is wrong or incomplete, amend it in the field. When it accurately reflects the investigation's target condition, click Confirm & Scope Data ↗.
Example: For the question "Why did The Surfaris' success fade after Wipe Out?", a correct Ideal Scene might be: "The Surfaris build on the runaway success of Wipe Out to achieve lasting chart presence, loyal fans, and a thriving career across the decade."
Step 3 — Confirm the Data Scope
Lensmen generates a scoped data brief: 8–12 specific categories of records, archives, and people relevant to your investigation. This is not a data dump — it is a focused list of what should be gathered before the lenses fire.
Add anything missing. Remove anything irrelevant. Always check that the full human network is represented — not just the named principals but everyone in orbit: managers, associates, rivals, family, journalists, anyone adjacent to the subject. Click Confirm & Apply Lenses ↗.
Step 4 — Read the Lens Analysis
16 lenses fire against the confirmed scope and Ideal Scene. Each produces a finding:
    • ⚠  Warn — a specific nuttiness is present and needs explaining
    • ?  Maybe — a possible nuttiness, one data point away from confirming
    • ~  Verify — no friction detected; this is what must be checked to confirm that holds

Click any finding to expand it. You will see the detail of the nuttiness and a specific next action — a person to locate, an archive to check, a record to cross-reference.
At the bottom of the analysis, the Primary Nuttiness identifies the single most senior outpoint: the one whose resolution is most likely to unlock the others. It includes a root cause hypothesis — not a verdict, but the most productive direction to investigate next.
Step 5 — Pull Threads
Every finding has a Pull thread ↗ button. The Threads to Pull summary box and the Primary Nuttiness block each have their own Pull this thread ↗ button. You can also type any thread into the input field at the bottom.
Each thread you pull runs through its own scoped data brief, lens analysis, and Primary Nuttiness — the same full process, focused on that specific outpoint. Multiple threads run in parallel. Open as many as the investigation warrants.

Status Indicators
    • ⚠  Red — Nuttiness: something specific doesn't add up. Pull this thread.
    • ?  Amber — Possible: friction detected but unconfirmed. One more data point needed.
    • ~  Green — Verify: no nuttiness found on this lens. Confirm what would need to be true.


Key Principles
    • Lensmen never concludes. Every finding points outward — toward an uninvestigated space, a person to find, a record to check.
    • The Ideal Scene is set before any analysis. Lenses measure deviation from the baseline — not from the shape of the question.
    • Data is scoped before lenses fire. The lenses work on real, defined material — not guesswork.
    • The human network matters. Absent testimony from someone who should have spoken is a finding. A relationship that should be documented and isn't is a finding.
    • Multiple threads run in parallel. Investigation does not wait for one thread to close before opening another.
